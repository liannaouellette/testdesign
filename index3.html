Traditions





Bar Mitzvah

 A bar mitzvah or bat mitzvah
is a coming-of-age ritual for young
  boys in Judaism. According to
Jewish law, before children reach
   a certain age, the parents
    are responsible for their
       child's actions.

Bat Mitzvah

     Becoming a bat mitzvah,
or “daughter of the commandments,”
   signifies that a young woman
    has attained legal adulthood
        under Jewish law.

Bris (Brit Milah)

    The brit milah is the
  ceremony of circumcision
        in Judaism.

Jewish Burial

    A Jewish burial will
   mainly consist of prayer,
hymns and religious readings.

Jewish Wedding

The ceremony includes two
    distinct rituals, the
betrothal (kiddushin) and the
  completion of the marriage
      itself (ni'usin).

Kashrut

Kashrut regulations that prohibit
    the eating of certain foods
     and require that other
    foods be prepared in a
        specified manner.
